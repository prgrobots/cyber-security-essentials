    What is CyberSecurity?

    5

    Definition: Cyber security refers to the protection of information systems (hardware, software and associated infrastructure), the data on them, and the services they provide, from unauthorised access, harm or misuse.

    This includes harm caused intentionally by the operator of the system, or accidentally, as a result of failing to follow security procedures. UKNationalCyberSecurityStrategy

    Information Security Goals – CIA T

    9

    All information security measures try to address at least one of three (or 4) goals: Protect the confidentiality of data Preserve the integrity of data Promote the availability of data for authorized use Trust by vendors and users. (Are you limiting YOUR online activities because you no longer fully trust the security of online systems?)

    OFFICIAL

    Confidentiality

    11

    Confidentiality is sometimes referred to as the principle of least privilege, meaning that users should be given only enough privilege to perform their duties, and no more. Common synonyms for confidentiality include privacy, secrecy, and discretion.

    Slide 12¶

    OFFICIAL

    Confidentiality

    12

    Confidentiality models are intended to block unauthorized access to information and make sure accidental disclosure of sensitive information is not possible. Common confidentiality controls are user IDs and passwords, 2 factor verification 2FV.

    Slide 13¶

    OFFICIAL

    Integrity models

    13

    Integrity models keep data pure and trustworthy by protecting system data from intentional or accidental changes. Integrity models have three goals: Prevent unauthorized users from making modifications to data or programs Prevent authorized users from making improper or unauthorized modifications Maintain internal and external consistency of data and programs

    Slide 14¶

    OFFICIAL

    Integrity models

    An example of integrity checks is balancing a batch of transactions to make sure that all the information is present and accurately accounted for.

    14

    Slide 15¶

    OFFICIAL

    Availability models

    Availability models keep data and resources available for authorized use, especially during emergencies or disasters.

    15

    Slide 16¶

    OFFICIAL

    Availability models

    16

    Three common challenges to availability: Denial of service (DoS) due to intentional attacks or because of undiscovered flaws in implementation (for example, a program written by a programmer who is unaware of a flaw that could crash the program if a certain unexpected input is encountered) Loss of information system capabilities because of natural disasters (fires, floods, storms, or earthquakes) or human actions (bombs or strikes) Equipment failures during normal use

    Bots

    24

    Bots are micro programs that can be designed to scan a system to find specific information. Bots are used widely for both good and bad Cyber criminals use bots to find credit card information, weak points in new software patches or previously unknown access points that can then be exploited. They are used in the early stages of an attack to locate the simplest method of access.

    Compliance with Cyber Security Policies

    25

    Does your workplace allow any of the following: signing into personal social media accounts from work machines? emailing sensitive data to work from a home machine or such data to your home machine? connecting company devices to unsecured networks? storing passwords on Word documents?

    Slide 26¶

    OFFICIAL

    Misuse Of Employee Privileges

    26

    The most common issues tend to be company devices being used for non-work purposes or else for work but in insecure locations. Preventative actions: blocking access to websites that pose a potential threat through malware or phishing, ensuring administrator privileges remain with the IT department, automated monitoring of device usage

    Slide 27¶

    OFFICIAL

    BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)

    27

    A study conducted by HP identified that 97% of employees’ devices contained privacy issues and 75% did not have sufficient data encryption. Storage devices;

    laptops, mobile phones, tablets and even wearable technology

    Slide 28¶

    OFFICIAL

    Staff training

    28

    Training ALL staff to be cyber aware and compliant with cyber security policies and procedures is essential. Provide ALL teams with the right skills to deal with potential threats before they mature into an imminent risk to your systems integrity.

    Slide 29¶

    OFFICIAL

    “Disaster” Recovery Planning

    29

    Assume one day the organization will suffer a successful cyber-attack. Post attack - obtain data from all network logs, error reports and personal observations that could indicate the cause of the data breach. Then review the data breach itself: What preventative measures were put in place to stop it What immediately followed the termination of the threat.

    Slide 30¶

    OFFICIAL

    Password Cracking

    30

    Generally accepted guidance when creating a strong password include: Don’t use the same password over and over for different applications. Avoid information about your family (for example: names or date of birth) Ensure all passwords are significantly different by adding unusual symbols.

    Slide 31¶

    OFFICIAL

    Ransomware

    Software designed to block access to a machine and files until a sum of money is paid. Ensure that critical files are backed up either continuously or very frequently. Keep your backup activity diversified and encrypt it so it makes unauthorized access harder to accomplish.
